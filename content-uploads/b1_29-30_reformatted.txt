Lesson 29 – Expressing Wishes & Hypothetical Meaning

Exercise A – Question Formation (8 questions)
Read each statement. Then write the correct question form using wish / if only / it's time / would rather.
1. Does Tom wish he spoke better English?
2. Does she wish she had studied harder for the exam?
3. If only they had more time, would they finish the project?
4. Isn't it time the children went to bed?
5. Would you rather I didn't use my phone during dinner?
6. Does he wish he hadn't said those unkind words?
7. Would she sooner her boss gave feedback in private?
8. If only we had left earlier, would we have missed the train?

Exercise B – Fill in the Blanks (8 questions)
Complete the gaps using the correct form of the words in brackets.
1. He _____ (wish / bring) his waterproof jacket.
2. He also wishes he _____ (book) a better tent.
3. If only we _____ (check) the weather forecast before we left!
4. By the third morning, everyone agreed it _____ (be) time they found a dry shelter.
5. The group leader said she _____ (would / rather / they / choose) an easier trail.
6. I'd sooner we _____ (not / give up) next time.
7. He just wishes he _____ (prepare) a little more carefully.
8. If only he _____ (read) the reviews of the campsite online.

Exercise C – Categorisation (10 questions)
Read each sentence. Write the letter of the structure it uses: A=wish+past simple, B=wish+past perfect, C=if only+past simple, D=it's time+past simple, E=would rather/sooner+past simple.
1. I wish I knew how to play the guitar.
2. She wishes she hadn't missed the meeting.
3. If only the bus came more often in this neighbourhood!
4. It's time you started saving money for the future.
5. I'd rather you told me the truth straightaway.
6. He wishes he could drive — it would make things easier.
7. If only we had booked the tickets sooner — they're sold out!
8. It's time the government did something about air pollution.
9. I'd sooner she applied for the job herself than wait.
10. They wish they had taken a different route to avoid the traffic.

Lesson 30 – Mixed Grammar Review & Error Analysis

Exercise A – Choose the Best Option (7 questions)
Select the correct answer for each sentence.
1. I _____ my homework when you called me yesterday. a) did b) was doing c) have done d) had done
2. She _____ to London three times. The last time _____ five months ago. a) has been / was b) has been / is c) went / was d) has gone / has been
3. By the time you arrive, I _____ dinner for two hours. a) will cook b) will be cooking c) will have been cooking d) have been cooking
4. They _____ a new office building since January. It _____ almost finished. a) have built / is b) have been building / is c) are building / has been d) built / is
5. You _____ to the meeting if you're too tired. It's not necessary. a) don't have to go b) mustn't go c) shouldn't go d) can't go
6. If I _____ you were moving house, I _____ you help! a) knew / would help b) had known / would have helped c) know / will help d) have known / can help
7. She _____ have left early; I saw her car in the parking lot an hour ago. a) can't b) shouldn't c) mustn't d) won't

3. My teacher told me that I _____ make more effort in class. a) have to b) had to c) must d) could
4. The accident _____ by a drunk driver. Three people _____ to hospital. a) was caused / were taken b) has caused / are taken c) was caused / was taken d) caused / have taken

6. The woman _____ sits next to me in class is a doctor. a) who b) whom c) whose d) which
